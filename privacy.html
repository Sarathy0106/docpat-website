Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 12, 2026

The short version: your medical documents are locked on your phone before they are uploaded anywhere, and only you hold the key. We — and every storage service we use — only ever see locked, unreadable data. We could not read your medical records even if we wanted to.

1. Who We Are

DocPat ("we", "us") is a private health record wallet that lets you store, verify, and share your medical documents securely. This policy explains what information the DocPat mobile application and its supporting services collect, how it is used, and the choices you have.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account information

  • Phone number — used for sign-in via one-time SMS passcode (OTP).
  • Email address and basic profile (optional) — if you choose to sign in with Google.
  • Account identifier (UID) — a random identifier created by our authentication provider.

2.2 Your medical documents — locked, always

When you upload a document (photo, scan, or PDF), it is locked on your device with strong, industry-standard protection before upload. Document details you enter — file name, document type, doctor name, hospital name, date, and notes — are also locked on your device before being stored. We have no technical ability to unlock or read any of this content. Your keys are kept in your phone's built-in secure storage and never leave your device in a usable form.

2.3 Activity and security records

  • Usage events — uploads, shares, views, deletions and key-recovery actions are recorded in an append-only audit log (metadata only, never document content).
  • Device records — the list of devices you have authorized, so you can manage multi-device access.
  • IP address and request data — processed by our backend for rate limiting, abuse prevention, and anomaly detection.
  • Login events — successful and failed sign-in attempts, used for security monitoring and brute-force protection.
  • Seal references — the reference codes created when your documents receive their permanent digital seal.

3. Where Your Data Lives

Data Stored In What that service can see
Document files A distributed storage network Locked, unreadable data only
Document details (names, notes, dates) Our secure cloud database Locked fields only
Document seal A public, permanent ledger A meaningless reference code and a date — no personal or medical information
Account & sign-in data Our sign-in provider Phone number, optional email, account ID
Activity records & safety alerts Our secure cloud database What happened and when — never document content
Key backup (optional) Our secure cloud database A locked backup that only your recovery PIN can open

4. Third-Party Services

DocPat relies on a small number of trusted service providers, each receiving only the minimum described above: a cloud platform for sign-in and database services, a distributed file-storage network that holds only locked files, a public ledger that holds only meaningless seal codes, and an on-device text-recognition service used when you scan a document. A current list of providers is available on request via the contact details below. We do not sell, rent, or trade your information to anyone. We do not use your data for advertising.

5. How We Use Information

  • To sign you in and sync your locked records across the devices you have authorized.
  • To give every document a permanent digital seal proving it existed, untouched, at a point in time.
  • To operate PIN-protected sharing, including approval requests, access logs, and revocation.
  • To protect the service — rate limiting, anomaly detection, brute-force lockouts, and breach defence.
  • To show you your own activity history.

6. Sharing Controlled by You

Documents are shared only when you generate a PIN-protected QR code, and only with the person who scans it, enters the correct PIN, and receives your explicit approval. Shares are time-limited, can be revoked by you at any moment, and recipients view documents in screenshot-protected sessions without permanent copies. Every access is recorded in your activity log.

7. Retention & Deletion

  • Documents — deleting a document marks it inactive and removes it from your wallet view. Because the storage network and the seal ledger are permanent by design, locked copies and seal codes may persist; they remain permanently unreadable without your keys.
  • Audit logs — kept in append-only form for security and integrity purposes; they cannot be edited, by us or anyone else.
  • Device records — deauthorized devices are retained in deactivated form for your audit trail.
  • Account deletion — you may request deletion of your account and associated personal data (phone number, email, profile) by contacting us at the address below.

8. Security Measures

Beyond locking your documents on your device, DocPat protects your account with PIN-protected recovery, fingerprint and face unlock, screenshot blocking on shared views, round-the-clock monitoring for unusual activity, permanent activity records that no one can edit, and the ability to freeze activity instantly in an emergency.

9. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, correct, export, or delete your personal data, and to object to or restrict certain processing. Because document content is locked with keys only you hold, the most complete export of your records is available directly in the app on your device. To exercise any right, contact us using the details below.

10. Children

DocPat is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as the product evolves (DocPat is currently in beta). Material changes will be announced in the app or on this page, with the "Last updated" date revised above.

12. Contact

Questions about privacy or data protection: docpat.contact@gmail.com — or visit our Contact page.
